World in 2025

Humans have enjoyed ruling the Earth for 300,000 years. However, for the first time since the Anthropocene epoch, humans have begun to worry about the rise of a new species—one that could become far more intelligent than humans: Artificial Intelligence.
Although AI has existed for over 60 years, the widespread concern began with the release of ChatGPT in December 2022. That moment marked a turning point, changing the world forever.

Initial frontier to Gen AI

ChatGPT initiated the race to AGI with GPT-3, maintaining a leading position for a significant period until the emergence of Anthropic's Claude. They now face competition from xAI's Grok and Google's Gemini. However, none of these companies anticipated that an open-source model, DeepSeek, from China—a country with less computing power than the U.S.—would outperform all these models across a range of benchmarks.
Falcon and Mistral started the trend of open-sourcing model weights. However, both were significantly outperformed by Meta's Llama-3. Zuck has already announced Llama-4, set to release in 2025. Meanwhile, Alibaba's Qwen is another promising model from China. Personally, I have used Qwen for its medical imaging capabilities, and it is truly impressive.
If I were ranking models based on my user experience, I would say: DeepSeek > Claude > ChatGPT.

Image Gen

Even though DALL-E existed before the arrival of ChatGPT, people only began to marvel at AI-generated images after seeing the results of MidJourney. Now, most state-of-the-art (SOTA) models can generate images because they can be easily fine-tuned on frameworks like Stable Diffusion or FLUX.

Video Gen

The tech world went berserk after seeing the demo of Sora in February 2024. However, as of December 2024, only the demo exists, and nothing has been officially released. Now, we have a lot of video-generation AIs, such as Kling (China), Runway ML, Pika Labs, and Luma (US). The impressive part is that open-source models like Mochi1 (US) and Hunyuan (China) are on par with the closed ones. Pepsi has already released an ad fully generated by AI, and I’ve created some good 30-second animated short films. I’m expecting fully AI-generated movies by 2025.

Speech, Music, Protein, SWE, RPA

Generative AI is not limited to just text, image, and video—it also includes speech, music, and protein-generation models. I can't comment much on these areas since I haven't tried them out myself. However, there are significant concerns about the potential misuse of these technologies. For instance, scam calls using cloned voices, face morphing for malicious purposes, and generating fake videos of crimes someone never committed are becoming real threats. Unfortunately, low-literacy individuals are likely to be the most affected by these advancements, as they may struggle to discern real content from AI-generated fakes.
Another important aspect to consider is the potential replacement of software engineering jobs. So far, most software engineering (SWE) tools haven't posed a significant threat to human developers. AI tools like GitHub Copilot and Cursor are primarily saving developers time rather than replacing them. The much-hyped Devin, for instance, has been released with a hefty monthly cost of $500 (₹42,000), making it inaccessible for many organizations. This means junior developers are unlikely to be replaced anytime soon, as the cost and limitations of these tools still make human expertise indispensable.
Anthropic's computer agent and Microsoft's UI Parser might also replace Robotic Process Automation tools like UiPath.
